Low Profile Thumb Prosthesis

This prosthesis is designed to provide a static thumb post for patients who have one or more fingers with a good range of motion, but no thumb to oppose against. The prosthesis consists of a silicone sleeve that goes around the patient's palm, tightened by a velcro strap. A mounting point for a static thumb post (optionally with a grippy silicone cover) is molded into the sleeve.

Assembly Instructions

Assemble Configuration and Rendering of 3D models

Things needed:

1 x 3D scan of patient's hand

Steps:

  1. Place a manifold, cleaned-up version of the patient's hand scan (if necessary, the model can be repaired using Netfabb) in the userScans folder.
  2. Open src/scad/configuration.scad in your favorite text editor.
  3. Type the filename of the patient's hand scan in place of "JudahLeftHandShortened.stl" in the configuration file.
  4. Open src/scad/alignment.scad using OpenSCAD so you can see the placement of the 3D model while you edit the configuration file. (you may have to manually press the Reload and Preview button every time you make a change to the configuration file)
  5. In configuration.scad, change the hand_d parameter to the diameter of the thickest part of the patient's hand in mm.
  6. Adjust the hand_pos (position of the hand in 3D space) and hand_rot (rotation of hand around each axis) parameters so that the 3D scan of the hand is aligned with the arrows as described in the following 2 steps.
  7. The palm of the hand should be roughly vertical, centered on the green crosshair as much as possible, and the hand should be oriented so that the patient's finger(s) flex toward the green arrow.
  8. The palmar surface should be just within the blue area when viewed from the side.
  9. (Optional) Adjust the other parameters in the configuration file to control the dimensions of various parts of the prosthesis. The default values should work most of the time.
  10. After you have aligned the hand, open src/scad/preview.scad in OpenSCAD to ensure that all parts of the prosthesis look correct.
  11. Open each part to be printed (from the src/scad folder) in OpenSCAD, render each one (F6) and export as STL, then print using the STL files. Recommended print settings are 0.2mm or smaller layer height, three perimeters, three top and bottom solid layers, 40% rectilinear or hexagonal infill, and support material enabled.

Assemble Palm Sleeve Mold Assembly

Things needed:

1 x Configuration and Rendering of 3D models
4 x M4 Hex Nut
1 x Mold - left half
1 x Rigid base with thumb
4 x Piece of Tape
1 x Mold - right half
4 x M4x15 Bolt

Steps:

  1. Set one half of the mold on a flat surface with the flat side down.
  2. Insert the rigid thumb base into the slot in the side of the mold, with the round part toward the inside and the shorter prong toward the closed end of the mold.
  3. Set the other half of the mold with the flat side up so the two halves align.
  4. Fasten the two halves together using the nuts and bolts in the holes at each corner.
  5. (Optional) use tape to seal the seams on the outside of the mold to prevent leakage.

Assemble Silicone sleeve

Things needed:

100 x grams of Dragon Skin 30 liquid silicone
1 x Palm Sleeve Mold Assembly
1 x Truncated Print of Patient's Hand
2 x Piece of Tape

Steps:

  1. Mix silicone according to the instructions from the supplier. Wear gloves and follow the supplier's safety guidelines while working with liquid silicone.
  2. Insert the truncated hand (automatically sized by truncated_hand.scad) in the opening of the mold, narrow side down, so that it sits in the center of the mold and lines up with the overall shape of the mold. Make sure there is a small gap between the truncated hand and the thumb base piece.
  3. Tape the truncated hand in place to prevent it from moving or floating when the silicone is poured. Leave some space around it open to pour the silicone and for air to escape.
  4. Pour the liquid silicone to fill the mold. Make sure it settles to the bottom and completely surrounds the printed hand.
  5. After the silicone cures (16 hours if using Dragon Skin 30), unscrew and separate the two halves of the mold, and pull out the truncated hand.
  6. If necessary, use a knife to cut off any excess silicone and/or cut the sleeve to better fit the patient's hand.

Assemble Thumb with Molded Grip

Things needed:

1 x Rigid base with thumb
10 x grams of Dragon Skin 10 liquid silicone
1 x Thumb Mold

Steps:

  1. Print the skeletal thumb and mold.
  2. Mix silicone according to the instructions from the supplier. Wear gloves and follow the supplier's safety guidelines while working with liquid silicone.
  3. Pour the liquid silicone to mostly fill the thumb mold.
  4. Insert the printed skeletal thumb into the filled mold as far as it will go.
  5. Remove most of the excess silicone. Small amounts of excess can be removed afer molding.
  6. After the silicone has cured (up to 7 hours if using Dragon Skin 10 Slow), carefully pull the thumb to remove it from the mold.
  7. If necessary, use a knife to trim off excess silicone so that the edges of the silicone part are flush with those of the hard printed part.

Assemble Low Profile Thumb Prosthesis

Things needed:

1 x Silicone sleeve
1 x Thumb with Molded Grip
1 x Velcro Strap

Steps:

  1. Insert the thumb into the slot on the base.
  2. Insert the velcro strap through the slot on the base.
  3. (Optional) If the velcro strap does not fit well around the silicone sleeve, use a knife to cut slits into the sides of the sleeve, then route the velcro sleeve through the slits.
  4. Insert patient's hand into the sleeve.
  5. Fasten the velcro strap and tighten as needed to hold the prosthesis in place during use.
