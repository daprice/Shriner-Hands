Assemble Palm Sleeve Mold Assembly

Things needed:

4 x M4 Hex Nut
1 x Mold - left half
1 x Rigid base
4 x Piece of Tape
1 x Mold - right half
4 x M4x15 Bolt

Steps:

  1. Set one half of the mold on a flat surface with the flat side down.
  2. Insert the rigid thumb base into the slot in the side of the mold, with the round part toward the inside and the shorter prong toward the closed end of the mold.
  3. Set the other half of the mold with the flat side up so the two halves align.
  4. Fasten the two halves together using the nuts and bolts in the holes at each corner.
  5. Optional: use tape to seal the seams on the outside of the mold to prevent leakage.

Assemble Silicone sleeve

Things needed:

100 x grams of Dragon Skin 30 liquid silicone
1 x Palm Sleeve Mold Assembly
1 x Truncated Print of Patient's Hand
2 x Piece of Tape

Steps:

  1. Mix silicone according to the instructions from the supplier. Wear gloves and follow the supplier's safety guidelines while working with liquid silicone.
  2. Insert the truncated hand in the opening of the mold, narrow side down, so that it sits in the center of the mold and lines up with the overall shape of the mold. Make sure there is a small gap between the truncated hand and the thumb base piece.
  3. Tape the truncated hand in place to prevent it from moving or floating when the silicone is poured. Leave some space around it open to pour the silicone and for air to escape.
  4. Pour the liquid silicone to fill the mold. Make sure it settles to the bottom and completely surrounds the printed hand.
  5. After the silicone cures, unscrew and separate the two halves of the mold, and pull out the truncated hand.
  6. If necessary, use a knife to cut off any excess silicone and/or cut the sleeve to better fit the patient's hand.

Assemble Thumb with Molded Grip

Things needed:

10 x grams of Dragon Skin 10 liquid silicone
1 x Thumb Mold
1 x Thumb with grip cutout

Steps:

  1. Print the skeletal thumb tip and mold.
  2. Mix silicone according to the instructions from the supplier. Wear gloves and follow the supplier's safety guidelines while working with liquid silicone.
  3. Pour the liquid silicone to mostly fill the thumb mold.
  4. Insert the printed skeletal thumb into the filled mold as far as it will go.
  5. Remove most of the excess silicone. Small amounts of excess can be removed afer molding.
  6. After the silicone has cured, carefully pull the thumb to remove it from the mold.
  7. If necessary, use a knife to trim off excess silicone so that the edges of the silicone part are flush with those of the hard printed part.

Assemble Assembly

Things needed:

1 x Silicone sleeve
1 x Thumb with Molded Grip
1 x Velcro Strap

Steps:

  1. Insert the thumb into the slot on the base.
  2. Insert the velcro strap through the slot on the base.
  3. Insert patient's hand into the sleeve.
  4. Fasten the velcro strap and tighten as needed to hold the prosthesis in place during use.
